Nature notes

Harebells have their own eponymous insect: the harebell carpenter bee. Only 7mm long, these black solitary bees are often mistaken for flies. For them, harebells are their world. They not only forage predominantly on this species, but these plants also form their mating ground. Males swarm round large colonies, searching for females. The pair then couple inside the bells. Not every gently nodding sky-blue cup is caused by the breeze. Males sleep inside the flowers too; the two-pronged peg on their abdomen giving them the necessary traction to hold on.
