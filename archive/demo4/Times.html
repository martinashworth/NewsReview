Nature notes

Houseflies, fruit flies and horseflies may plague us at this time of year, but hoverflies, known in America as flower flies, are welcome visitors to gardens. These agile, stingless, pollen-munching creatures are vital pollinators, and the larvae of many kinds are capable of eating aphids at an astonishing rate. There are between 250 and 300 species in Britain (expert opinion varies), some widespread, some found in only four locations, and ranging from a whopping 2cm to the size of an ant. Most have only a Latin moniker, though a few, including the huge hornet mimic, the stripy marmalade or the large pied, are common enough, or striking enough, to have picked up common names.
